## LinkedIn about section (draft)

I design learning systems that help organizations close the gap between what they intend and what their environments produce.

My practice is grounded in adult learning theory, the science of learning and instruction, Universal Design for Learning, and evidence from the cognitive, motivational, and sociocultural dimensions of learning — applied at the organizational systems level.

What this looks like in practice: diagnosing why learning environments aren't working (not just what programs are missing), designing for sustainability and equity from the outset, and working with the full complexity of organizations — their cultures, their constraints, and their people.

I've done this work in higher education (UBC, Emily Carr, BCIT), research (TRIUMF), and the public sector (ICBC, provincial government). Each context has different vocabulary, different constraints, and different definitions of what "good learning" means. That cross-sector range is part of what I bring.

Currently open to learning design or L&D specialist roles in higher education, research institutions, or the public sector — remote or hybrid, Vancouver, BC.
