# Outreach message templates

These are starting points — always customize before sending.
Tone principle: warm, specific, non-desperate. You are reaching out because you have something to offer and a genuine interest. Not because you need a favour.

---

## Warm contact — former colleague

**Use when:** You've worked with this person and want to reconnect as part of your search.

**Subject:** Reconnecting — and a question if you have 20 minutes

> Hi [Name],
>
> I hope you're well. It's been [time] since we worked together at [org] — I still think about [specific thing: the project / the approach you took / something they taught you].
>
> I'm in the middle of a deliberate job search right now, looking for a learning design or L&D role in [higher education / research / public sector] — ideally remote or hybrid in Vancouver. I'm not asking you to flag anything specific, but I'd genuinely value a 20-minute conversation to hear how you're thinking about this sector and whether there's anyone you'd suggest I talk to.
>
> Happy to work around your schedule — and of course, happy to return the favour if there's ever anything I can help with.
>
> [Andrea]

## Warm contact — adjacent field, weaker connection

**Use when:** You know this person peripherally — a conference, a shared project, a mutual contact.

**Subject:** [Mutual connection or shared context] — a quick question

> Hi [Name],
>
> We met briefly at [context], or [mutual contact] suggested I reach out to you.
>
> I'm a learning design specialist currently looking for my next role — focused on [higher education / research / public sector] in Vancouver. I've been following [their work / their organization] and have real respect for [specific thing].
>
> I'm not job hunting in your direction specifically, but I'd love to spend 20 minutes learning from your perspective on the sector. Would you be open to a short call sometime in the next few weeks?
>
> [Andrea]
> [andrearobin.com]

## Cold outreach — specific org, no connection

**Use when:** You want to reach a hiring manager or team lead at a target org where you have no warm connection.

**Subject:** Learning design inquiry — [your name]

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm a learning design specialist with [X] years of experience working across higher education, research institutions, and the public sector — including [relevant former employer]. I came across [organization] through [specific: a project they published / a posting that's now closed / a recommendation] and have been genuinely interested in the work you're doing in [specific area].
>
> I'm currently exploring my next role and wanted to reach out directly rather than wait for a posting. If you'd be open to a brief conversation about the work your team does and where learning design sits in the organization, I'd welcome it.
>
> I've attached a brief overview of my background, and case studies are at [andrearobin.com].
>
> [Andrea]

## Follow-up after applying

**Use when:** You've submitted an application and want to signal genuine interest without being pushy.

**Subject:** Re: [Role title] application — Andrea Robin

> Hi [Name],
>
> I submitted an application for the [role title] position on [date] and wanted to follow up briefly.
>
> I'm genuinely interested in this role — [one specific sentence about why this org]. I'm happy to provide any additional materials or context that would be useful.
>
> Thank you for your time.
>
> [Andrea]

---

## Thank you after an interview

**Use when:** Within 24 hours of an interview.

**Subject:** Thank you — [Role title] conversation

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for the conversation today. I appreciated [something specific that came up — a question they asked, something you learned about the team, a connection you made].
>
> Our discussion about [specific topic] reinforced my sense that [brief genuine statement about the role or org]. I remain very interested.
>
> Please don't hesitate to reach out if there's anything further I can provide.
>
> [Andrea]
