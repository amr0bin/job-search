# Master cover letter structure

This is the modular architecture. Each sector version is built from these blocks.
Swap blocks as needed — don't rewrite from scratch each time.

---

## Block A — Opening (sector-specific)

### Higher education version
> [Organization]'s commitment to [specific initiative or value from their website] reflects something I've observed consistently across my work in higher education: when learning environments are designed with genuine intention — not just content delivery in mind — they produce measurably different outcomes for learners and institutions alike. I'm applying for the [role title] position because this work is the centre of my practice.

### Public sector version
> Organizational change without learning infrastructure to support it rarely holds. I've watched this dynamic play out across my work in the public sector — at ICBC, in provincial government contexts, and in large-scale workforce transitions — and it's what draws me to [organization]'s [role title] position. You're working on something that matters, and the learning design behind it matters too.

### Research institution version
> Research institutions produce knowledge that can change things — but only if the people inside them, and the communities around them, can access and apply it. Designing the learning infrastructure that makes that possible is the work I've done at TRIUMF, in higher education, and across the public sector. I'm applying for the [role title] role at [organization] because this is exactly where I want that work to continue.

### Cultural org version
> Public learning — the kind that happens outside classrooms, in spaces people choose to enter — requires a different design sensibility than formal education. It has to earn attention, build on what people bring, and leave them with something real. That understanding is what I bring to [organization]'s [role title] position, along with [X] years of learning design practice across informal and formal settings.

## Block B — What I bring (choose 2–3 threads)

### Thread: Systems-level design
> My practice operates at the systems level — not just program design, but the organizational conditions that make learning work or prevent it from working. At [former org context], this meant [brief specific example: a province-wide curriculum for a workforce transition / an evaluation framework for a national research institution / an equitable experiential learning model at scale]. The work involved [needs analysis / stakeholder co-design / evaluation design], and the result was [brief outcome].

### Thread: Equity as design practice
> Equity integration isn't a layer I add after design — it's a structural practice embedded from the first conversation about what learning needs to accomplish. This means applying Universal Design for Learning as a systems framework, identifying barriers at the point of design, and building for the full range of learners rather than the imagined average. At [context], this produced [brief outcome].

### Thread: Research orientation
> I approach learning design as applied science: what does the evidence say about how people learn in this context, for this purpose? That orientation shapes how I conduct needs analysis, how I design assessments, and how I build evaluation into programs rather than onto them. My background in neurobiology and public health gives me genuine fluency with research methodology — useful when working alongside researchers or in evidence-intensive environments.

### Thread: Cross-sector translation
> I've designed learning systems in higher education, research, and the public sector — each with different cultures, constraints, and definitions of what "good learning" means. That range means I can translate across contexts, recognize sector-specific patterns quickly, and avoid the mistakes of someone importing one sector's assumptions into another.

## Block C — Why this org (must be customized — no template)

This block MUST be written fresh for each application.
It should reference something specific you know about this organization that a generic applicant wouldn't know.

**Research prompts:**
- What specific initiative, program, or commitment on their website drew your attention?
- What do you know about their learning culture from former colleagues or public sources?
- What gap or opportunity do you see in their current learning infrastructure?
- What in their mission connects directly to your own values?
