# Search timeline and weekly rhythm

---

## Phases and realistic timeframes

| Phase | Duration | What's happening |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 1 — Foundation | ✅ Complete | Website, case studies, assets ready |
| 2 — Intelligence | 2–3 weeks | Org research, salary benchmarking, sector vocabulary |
| 3 — Activation | 4–8 weeks | Warm outreach active, applications 3–5/week |
| 4 — Interviews | 2–6 weeks | Interview prep, active conversations |
| 5 — Offer | 1–2 weeks | Evaluate, negotiate, decide |

**Realistic timeline to offer:** 2–5 months from activation, depending on market and timing.
Higher education and BC public sector hiring can be slow — build in patience.

---

## Weekly rhythm

A sustainable search rhythm. Adjust based on your current employment status.

### Monday — Research and planning (45–60 min)
- Review Airtable for follow-ups needed
- Check job boards (see `research/job-board-sources.md`)
- Add any new postings to Airtable
- Plan the week's outreach targets

### Wednesday — Writing and outreach (60–90 min)
- Draft or send outreach messages
- Work on applications in progress
- Update org research files

### Friday — Review and log (30 min)
- Update PROGRESS.md with week's activity
- Note any new intelligence gathered
- Award XP
- Set Monday priorities

---

## Monthly rhythm

**First week of each month:**
- Review all Tier 1 org websites for new postings
- Check in with 1–2 contacts you haven't spoken with in a while
- Assess: what's working in your outreach? What's not?

**Quarterly:**
- Review and update positioning.md — is it still accurate?
- Update sector-vocabulary.md with any new language you're encountering in JDs
- Refresh stories-bank.md based on interviews so far

## Seasonal notes for BC hiring

**Higher education:**
- Most hiring happens January–March and August–October
- Avoid submitting during exam periods (April, August) — slower response
- Budget cycles often dictate when new roles are approved (April = new fiscal year)

**BC public sector:**
- Hiring is year-round but can slow in July–August
- New fiscal year (April 1) often releases approved headcount
- Competition processes are slow — 6–10 weeks from posting to offer is normal

**Research institutions:**
- Grant cycles drive hiring — watch for major announcements
- Summer hiring is common as new grant years begin

## Morale maintenance

Job searching is inherently demoralizing. Build in protection:

- **Cap active search time** at 2 hours/day maximum when employed; 4 hours when not
- **Do not check email obsessively** — set two check-in times per day for job search correspondence
- **Celebrate effort milestones**, not just outcomes: first 5 applications sent, first outreach conversation, etc.
- **Maintain at least one non-search professional activity** — writing, a project, a community — to keep your sense of identity separate from the search
- **Talk to someone** (a friend, a mentor, a coach) every 2–3 weeks — not to strategize, but to process
