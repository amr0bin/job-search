# Positioning

This is the strategic foundation. When in doubt about how to describe yourself, come back here.

---

## Core positioning statement

I design learning systems that help organizations close the gap between what they intend and what their environments produce — coherently, equitably, and with lasting effect.

---

## What makes me different

Most L&D professionals work at the program level: design a course, run a workshop, build an LMS module. I work at the systems level — diagnosing why learning isn't working, identifying structural barriers, and designing for sustainability and equity from the outset.

Three things make this positioning credible and rare:

1. **Cross-sector depth.** I've done this work in higher education, research, and public sector settings — each with different cultures, constraints, and definitions of "learning." I can translate across those contexts.

2. **A research and evidence orientation.** My background in neurobiology and public health means I approach learning design as an applied science, not a creative exercise. Needs analysis, evaluation frameworks, and evidence-based design are how I work, not extras I add at the end.

3. **Equity as a design practice, not a compliance layer.** Universal Design for Learning, systemic barrier identification, and accessible design are integrated into my process — not a checklist applied after the fact.

## What I am not positioning myself as

- An LMS administrator
- A corporate training facilitator
- An e-learning developer (Articulate/Storyline-first)
- An HR generalist who does some training

If a job description is primarily about these things, it is not the right role.

## Salary positioning

The $100k+ target is consistent with senior/specialist L&D roles in BC public sector, academic teaching and learning centres, and research institutions. This is not an inflated ask — it reflects 15+ years of applied work at a systems level across multiple sectors.

When salary comes up:
- Name a range: "$100,000–$115,000, depending on scope and benefits"
- Anchor high within the posted band if one is given
- Benefits (extended health, dental, pension/RRSP) are part of the total compensation conversation
