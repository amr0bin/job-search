# Role fit rubric

Score any role against these six criteria before applying. Derived from your O*NET work values and interests profile.
**Threshold:** 4/6 minimum to apply. 5+/6 to prioritize.

---

## The six criteria

### 1. Social impact is the actual mission
_Work interest: Social_

The org exists to help people — not just as a values statement. Learning is in service of something that matters beyond the institution.

**Green flags:** Public mandate · equity-centred language · external communities served
**Red flags:** Internal training only · profit-driven parent org · compliance-first culture

Score: __ / 1

---

### 2. Research and inquiry are valued
_Work interest: Investigative_

The role involves diagnosing problems, not just executing solutions. Evidence, needs analysis, and evaluation are in the job description.

**Green flags:** Needs assessment mentioned · evaluation framework · "learning science" language
**Red flags:** "Deliver training" only · no mention of assessment · "off-the-shelf content"

Score: __ / 1

---

### 3. Design and creative thinking are expected
_Work interest: Artistic_

You're hired to design, not administer. The role has room for original thinking about structure, experience, and form.

**Green flags:** "Design" in title · portfolio requested · co-design processes mentioned
**Red flags:** "Facilitate existing programs" only · no portfolio ask · heavy LMS admin focus

Score: __ / 1

---

### 4. Collegial, non-competitive team
_Work value: Relationships_

Team culture is collaborative and grounded in shared values, not performative or status-driven.

**Green flags:** Small team · long staff tenure · "we work closely with" language
**Red flags:** High turnover signals · "fast-paced, results-driven" without warmth · siloed structure

Score: __ / 1

---

### 5. Ability utilization — you lead the work
_Work value: Achievement_

Your strongest capabilities are actually used. The role is scoped at your level — systems thinking, not task execution.

**Green flags:** Senior/specialist title · "lead" or "design" the strategy · cross-functional scope
**Red flags:** Coordinator title at your experience level · reporting to someone with less L&D expertise

Score: __ / 1

---

### 6. Autonomy is structural, not just stated
_Work value: Independence_

You have genuine ownership over your work — methodology, approach, timelines. Built into the role, not just a culture claim.

**Green flags:** Project ownership · remote/hybrid offered · flexible deliverable structure
**Red flags:** "Follows established procedures" · rigid process adherence · micromanagement signals

Score: __ / 1
