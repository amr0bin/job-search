## Benefits to factor into total compensation

At $100k base, look for:
- Extended health (dental, vision, paramedical — $2,000–$5,000/year value)
- Pension or RRSP matching (3–5% employer contribution = $3,000–$5,000/year)
- Professional development budget ($1,500–$3,000/year)
- Vacation (3–4 weeks minimum; public sector often starts at 3, scales up)
- Sick leave (public sector often has generous sick leave)

**Total compensation at $100k base with full benefits:** ~$115,000–$120,000 equivalent

---

## Negotiation notes

- If a range is posted, name the top third of the range in your ask
- If no range is posted: "I'm targeting $100,000–$110,000, depending on the full benefits package"
- Never be the first to name a number if you can avoid it
- Benefits and professional development budget are negotiable even when salary isn't
- In BC public sector, salary is often fixed by band — negotiate the band level, not within it
- Always ask about the timeline to step increases if hired below your target
