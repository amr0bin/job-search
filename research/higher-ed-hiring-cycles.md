# Higher education hiring cycles — BC

Understanding when universities hire helps you time outreach and applications strategically.

---

## General patterns

Higher education hiring is **cyclical and budget-dependent**. Most institutions:
- Operate on a fiscal year of May 1 – April 30 (some use April 1)
- Post most positions after budget approval (January–March for spring/summer starts)
- Have a second hiring wave in August–September for fall starts
- Slow significantly during exam periods (April, August) and summer (July–August for admin roles)

---

## By institution type

### Teaching and Learning Centres (highest fit for your profile)
These units support faculty development and instructional design. They are often:
- Small teams (3–12 people) with low turnover
- Funded through central university budgets — hiring follows approval cycles
- Most active in January–April and August–October

**Key units to watch:**
- UBC Centre for Teaching, Learning and Technology (CTLT)
- SFU Teaching and Learning Centre
- BCIT Learning and Teaching Centre
- Kwantlen Centre for Academic Growth and Support
- Royal Roads Centre for Teaching and Educational Technologies

### Faculty of Education / School of Education
Educational development roles sometimes sit here rather than in central CTLs.

### Continuing Studies / Professional Development units
Often hire instructional designers for online and professional education programs.
These units may have more budget flexibility and move faster than academic units.

### Accessibility Services / Student Services
If a role involves UDL or accessibility design, it may be posted here.

## Notes on UBC specifically (largest employer in your sector)

- UBC has multiple HR streams: excluded management, CUPE 2950, IUOE — role type determines which applies
- Teaching and Learning roles are typically excluded management or PEA (Professional Employees Association)
- PEA positions tend to have better pay and more autonomy than support staff roles
- CTLT hires periodically — set a job alert AND check the portal monthly
- Former UBC employment (yours) is noted positively in the system and by hiring managers
