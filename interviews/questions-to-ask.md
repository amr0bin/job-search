# Questions to ask in interviews

These questions do two things: gather real intelligence about fit, and signal that you think at the systems level.
Prepare 6–8 per interview. You'll typically ask 4–5.

---

## About the role and scope

- What does success look like in this role at 6 months? At 12?
- How much of the work is design vs. delivery vs. administration? What's the balance currently, and what would you want it to be?
- Who does this role collaborate with most closely — and who tends to be hardest to work with?
- Is there an existing learning strategy, or is part of this role to build one?
- What's the biggest unsolved problem in learning design at this organization right now?

---

## About autonomy and culture (probing your Independence value)

- How are decisions made about learning priorities? Who sets them?
- What does it look like when someone in this role has a strong opinion about methodology that differs from how things have been done?
- Can you give me an example of something this team changed based on evidence or evaluation?
- How would you describe the management style of the person this role reports to?

---

## About equity and inclusion (probing your values fit)

- How does the organization approach accessibility in its learning programs — and where is that work currently?
- Has there been a time where a learning program didn't work as well for some groups as others? How was that identified and addressed?
- Where does equity and inclusion show up in how learning is designed here, beyond compliance requirements?

---

## About team and relationships (probing your Relationships value)

- How long have people on this team been in their roles?
- How does the team typically make decisions together?
- What's the thing people on this team most often disagree about?
- How has the team changed in the last two years?

---

## About the organization's learning culture

- How does leadership here understand the role of learning and development? Is it seen as strategic or operational?
- What's the appetite for trying things that are new or untested?
- Are there examples of learning programs or initiatives from the last few years that you're most proud of?

---

## Closing question (always ask this one)

- Is there anything about my background or experience that gives you pause — anything you'd want to understand better before moving forward?

_This question is powerful because it surfaces objections while you're still in the room to address them._
